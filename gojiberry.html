TL;DR

Gojiberry is a Shopify app that helps e-commerce store owners collect and analyze zero-party data through various types of surveys and product quizzes directly from their customers. It allows businesses to gather direct insights from their customers, enabling data-driven decisions to improve customer experience, product development, and marketing strategies.

My Role

As co-founder, I lead end-to-end design, conduct user research, develop product roadmaps, go-to market strategies, pitch to investors, and doubled our team from 4 to 8 members.

The Problem

Without interacting with customers physically, a lot of context is lost
In the post-COVID era, as more brands transition to e-commerce, they face significant challenges in maintaining meaningful connections with their customers. The absence of physical interaction leads to a loss of context, creating a gap in understanding consumer needs.

Decisions made based on guesswork
This lack of direct communication results in brands relying on guesswork to make decisions. Additionally, managing data sources becomes increasingly difficult, with information often being unreliable and unactionable.

Customer research is costly and requires domain knowledge
Furthermore, conducting user research remains a complex and costly endeavor, making it harder for brands to gain valuable insights into their customer base.

Information architecture

Gojiberry’s architecture is divided into two main components: the Survey tool and the Quiz tool. The Survey tool allows store owners to create, manage, and analyze surveys through an intuitive flow, from defining the content and incentives to customizing and previewing the survey. The Quiz tool, currently in beta, operates independently, enabling the creation and management of personalized quizzes with a focus on content, recommendations, styling, and previewing. Although distinct, both tools share a similar project-based structure which ensures our current survey users a smooth onboarding to the quiz feature as well.

Survey

Gojiberry's survey feature is a tool that helps online businesses gather feedback directly from their customers. Store owners can easily create surveys to understand customer preferences, experiences, and needs. The tool makes it simple to embed these surveys on different parts of a website or share them through links. The data collected helps businesses make informed decisions to improve their products and services. This feature is designed to be user-friendly, even for those without technical expertise, providing valuable insights to drive business growth.

Quiz

Gojiberry's quiz feature further connects zero-party customer data with actionable insights. It allows businesses not only to collect important customer information that can inform the store’s persona but also to drive customer engagement and increase sales by offering tailored product recommendations. By integrating this data with broader marketing strategies, the quiz feature not only improves customer experience but also contributes to more effective and targeted business growth.

Our project experienced 2 major pivot points to get to where we are today. I’m going to break it down into 3 stages:

Stage 1 (2022.09 ~ 2023.01)

How might we empower the product development process by connecting small business owners with their customers?

Finding the key pain points

In today's digital landscape, gathering genuine customer feedback can be a significant challenge. The tools available often fail to provide clear insights into what customers truly think, making it difficult to identify and address problems effectively. Without direct and actionable feedback, businesses are left in the dark, unsure of what aspects of their product or service may not be resonating with their audience. This lack of clarity hinders informed decision-making and can stall growth and improvement efforts.

1-on-1 interviews

We've developed a 1-on-1 interview scheduling tool for Shopify, available for private beta testing. This tool allows users to choose which customers they wish to interview and select their preferred time slots. Once the choices are made, the tool generates a message and handles the scheduling process with the selected customers.

Results and learnings

Our first prototype was a 1-on-1 interview scheduling tool designed for Shopify merchants to engage directly with their customers. Despite securing a few beta testers, the tool did not gain traction as merchants either lacked time or had highly specific and varied interview methods, ranging from in-depth, hour-long sessions to brief 15-minute calls or recruiting power users.

As we reflected on our overall strategy, we analyzed potential areas where issues might arise. Our strategy consists of five layers: target user, problem definition, solution, usability, and marketing. Given our success in finding users and conducting usability tests, we believe the primary challenge may lie in achieving a strong problem-solution fit.

stage 2 (2022.01 ~ 2023.06)

How might we empower business owners to improve on their store with customer insights, and to do so effortlessly?

Finding the problem-solution fit

The biggest reason most store owners rejected to participate in our beta is that they had no time to do so. With such assumption, we quickly made a pitch and showed it to a couple store owners that matched with our persona.

Surveys

Simple survey with templates that covers the main areas that most store owners want to learn about, including marketing attribution, NPS, usability etc.

There’s also multiple ways a store owner could incentivize their customers, by discount or by loyalty points.

Store owners can effortlessly view aggregated survey results on our dashboard. Additionally, our platform provides performance data and benchmarks, enabling users to refine and enhance their survey designs.

Surveys could be displayed on all touch points in a customer’s shopping journey, from pre-purchase with our in-store widget, to post-purchase with the thank you and order status page embed or survey link.

Results and learnings

We successfully gain traction and grew 300% over last year. After 8 months of public beta, we launched our pricing plan in June 2024. This has proven that we’ve built a feature that people are willing to pay for.

stage 3 (2022.06 ~ Now)

How might we empower business owners to collect, analyze, and act on customer insights, and do so as a habit?

Finding the product-market fit

Store owners often lack insights into their store's persona and face challenges due to limited face time, impacting growth and causing inventory issues. With 81% of brands valuing personalized experiences, current guesswork in decision-making is problematic. Additionally, the need to collect email leads through discounts, which negatively affects profit margins, is a concern for many store owners.

Product quiz

Our product quiz gathers valuable data to optimize marketing campaigns and guide product development, leading to better inventory management. It also captures email leads without requiring a coupon, enhancing the personalized shopping experience for customers.

Simple interface to build quiz questions first.

Link the quiz questions and options with products to build a recommendation logic.

Customize quiz design to match with brand and store visuals.

Substantial quiz templates to easily get started.

Current traction

In general, the quiz has received well among our current users and during our user research. Over 80% of users we interviewed said that they would love to try out product quiz in their store. We are currently building the private beta version and we are running the beta version with 2 of our existing customers who’s willing to try out the feature.
